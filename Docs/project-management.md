# Project Management

## Purpose

Project Management handles the complete lifecycle of client engagements from initial lead through successful delivery. **Project Managers** are the primary client stakeholders and own end-to-end project delivery.

---

## Project Concept

A **project** is a bounded engagement with a specific client that has defined:
- **Scope**: What will be delivered
- **Timeline**: When it will be delivered
- **Deliverables**: Specific outputs and acceptance criteria
- **Team**: Who will execute the work
- **Budget**: Cost and resource allocation

Each project is assigned to a dedicated **Project Manager** who serves as the primary client contact and owns end-to-end project delivery.

---

## Project Lifecycle

### Phase 1: Lead Generation & Initial Contact

**Who**: Marketing Manager → Project Manager

**Timeline**: Days 1-3 of opportunity

**Key Activities**:
- Marketing team identifies qualified client opportunity through lead generation channels
- Opportunity assessment (fit, requirements, feasibility)
- Routing to appropriate Project Manager (based on capacity and expertise)

**Project Manager Responsibilities**:
- Conduct initial discovery call with client understanding business needs and pain points
- Discuss high-level requirements and potential scope
- Estimate preliminary timeline and resource needs
- Determine viability and assess client fit
- Prepare internal opportunity assessment

**Deliverable**: Initial Opportunity Assessment Document
- Client profile and context
- Preliminary scope understanding
- Estimated timeline range
- Resource requirements estimate
- Recommendation (proceed/no-go)

---

### Phase 2: Requirement Gathering

**Who**: Project Manager

**Process**:
- Schedule detailed requirements meeting with client
- Document client needs, goals, and constraints
- Define project scope explicitly (including exclusions)
- Capture functional and technical requirements
- Establish timeline and milestones
- Define acceptance criteria for deliverables
- Identify risks and assumptions
- Document any special constraints

**Deliverable**: Requirements document with approval from client

---

### Phase 3: Project Setup & Planning

**Who**: Project Manager

**Process**:
- Create project record in system
- Record all client information
- Document approved requirements
- Set project timeline and milestones
- Allocate budget and resources
- Create project folder/repository
- Set up communication channels
- Establish status update schedule

**Deliverable**: Project setup complete in system

---

### Phase 4: Team Assignment

**Who**: Project Manager + Executive Officer

**Process**:
- Identify team needed (size, skills, experience)
- Request team creation from Executive Officer (if needed)
- Assign or create Team Lead
- Identify and assign team members
- Define team structure and roles
- Establish internal communication protocol
- Schedule project kickoff meeting

**Deliverable**: Team ready and assigned to project

---

### Phase 5: Task Breakdown & Planning

**Who**: Project Manager + Team Lead

**Process**:
- Project Manager creates high-level project tasks
- Team Lead reviews and breaks into daily subtasks
- Align task timeline with project milestones
- Assign initial tasks to team members
- Distribute workload appropriately
- Document dependencies
- Schedule project kickoff with team

**Deliverable**: Task list created and assigned

---

### Phase 6: Execution & Monitoring

**Who**: Team Lead (execution) + Project Manager (oversight)

**Process**:
- Team executes daily tasks per plan
- Team Lead monitors progress and quality
- Project Manager communicates with client per schedule
- Status updates provided to client on schedule
- Issues and risks escalated immediately
- Project Manager makes scope/timeline adjustments (with client approval)
- Weekly or bi-weekly status check-ins

**Deliverable**: Consistent project progress

---

### Phase 7: Milestone Delivery

**Who**: Team + Project Manager

**Process**:
- Team completes milestone deliverables
- Deliverables compiled and reviewed
- Client reviews and accepts deliverables
- Client feedback documented
- Next phase or final delivery planned
- Payment processed (if applicable)

**Deliverable**: Accepted deliverables delivered to client

---

### Phase 8: Project Completion

**Who**: Project Manager + Admin

**Process**:
- All deliverables accepted by client
- Final payment received (if applicable)
- Team performance evaluated
- Final report and lessons learned documented
- Project archived
- Team dissolved or reassigned
- Client feedback collected

**Deliverable**: Project archived with completion documentation

---

## Project Fields & Documentation

**Project Record** includes:
- **Project Name**: Clear, descriptive name
- **Client Name**: Company or individual
- **Client Contact**: Primary contact person, email, phone
- **Project Description**: Purpose and business context
- **Scope**: What will be delivered (explicit)
- **Out of Scope**: What will NOT be delivered (explicit)
- **Deliverables**: Specific outputs with acceptance criteria
- **Timeline**: Start date, end date, key milestones
- **Budget**: Total allocated cost or resource hours
- **Team**: Assigned team and members
- **Project Manager**: Point of contact
- **Status**: Prospecting → Qualified → Planning → Active → On Hold → Completed
- **Requirements Document**: Link to detailed specifications
- **Risk Log**: Known risks and mitigation plans
- **Change Log**: Approved changes to scope/timeline/budget

---

## Project Manager Responsibilities

### Strategic Responsibility
- **Primary Client Contact**: Only authorized communicator with client
- **Requirement Owner**: Responsible for clear, documented requirements
- **Timeline Advocate**: Defend realistic timelines vs. client pressure
- **Quality Advocate**: Ensure team delivers per acceptance criteria
- **Risk Manager**: Identify and mitigate project risks

### Operational Responsibility
- **Team Formation**: Create team, assign roles
- **Task Oversight**: Monitor task creation and assignment
- **Communication**: Regular client updates (weekly or per agreement)
- **Escalation**: Escalate issues to Executive Officer promptly
- **Documentation**: Maintain project records and decisions

### Reporting Responsibility
- **Status Reports**: Daily/weekly summaries to Admin/Exec Officer
- **Performance Tracking**: Monitor team delivery and quality
- **Financial Reporting**: Track budget vs. actual
- **Risk Reporting**: Escalate risks and mitigations
- **Lessons Learned**: Post-project review

---

## Client Communication

### Authorization
- Project Manager: **Full authorization** to communicate with client
- Team Lead: **By exception only** with explicit Project Manager permission
- Developers: **Not authorized** to communicate with clients
- Interns: **Not authorized** to communicate with clients

### Communication Channels
- **Email**: Official channel (archived and tracked)
- **Project Management System**: Preferred for transparency
- **Phone Calls**: Documented in system, scheduled calls
- **Meetings**: Structured with agendas and minutes

### Client Contact Information
- Stored centrally in system
- Updated as needed
- Shared with team on need-to-know basis
- Protected from unauthorized access

### Communication Standards
- Professional tone and language
- Response time: Within 24 business hours
- Monthly or bi-weekly status updates (per agreement)
- Critical issues escalated same day
- All decisions documented and confirmed in writing

## Task-to-Project Relationship

```
PROJECT (Client engagement)
  └─ PROJECT TASKS (High-level, 2-5 days each)
      └─ TEAM TASKS (Daily breakdown, 4-8 hours each)
          └─ DAILY WORK (Specific assignments)
```

**Linkage Benefits**:
- Project-level reporting aggregates task data
- Milestone achievement measured through task completion
- Client deliverables map to completed project tasks
- Time tracking at task level provides project time data
- Quality metrics roll up to project level

## Client & Legal Compliance

### Pre-Project
- Client Service Agreement signed
- Scope of Work approved
- Payment terms defined
- IP and ownership terms established
- NDA if applicable
- Insurance and liability terms

### During Project
- Requirements changes documented and approved
- Client communication tracked
- Deliverable acceptance criteria clear
- Payment schedule honored
- Compliance maintained

### Post-Project
- Final deliverables accepted
- All documentation delivered
- IP transferred as per agreement
- Final payment received
- Project data archived

---

## Change Management

### Scope Change Request
- Client requests change or modification
- Project Manager estimates impact (scope, timeline, cost)
- Submit change request to Admin approval
- If approved: Execute change order
- If denied: Document reason and communicate to client

### Timeline Change
- If behind schedule: Assess cause and mitigation
- May require additional resources or scope reduction
- Approve with Executive Officer
- Communicate revised timeline to client

### Budget Change
- If over budget: Document reason
- Request Executive Officer approval
- Adjust client billing if applicable
- Document decision

### Change Order Process
1. Change identified
2. Impact analysis completed
3. Client approves change
4. Change order signed
5. Implementation begins
6. Documented in project record

## Project Reporting

### Daily Status
- Team Lead submits daily task progress to Project Manager
- Any risks or blockers flagged
- Any client communication updates

### Weekly Project Report
- Tasks completed vs. planned
- Milestone progress
- Risk status
- Budget vs. actual
- Client feedback
- Next week's plan

### Monthly Executive Report
- Overall portfolio performance
- Key metrics across projects
- Issues and resolutions
- Financial status
- Client satisfaction
- Recommendations

### Final Project Report
- Project completion summary
- Deliverables accepted
- Final financial accounting
- Team performance evaluation
- Lessons learned
- Recommendations for future projects

## Stakeholder Management

**Key Stakeholders**:
- **Client**: Ultimate authority on deliverables and acceptance
- **Team Lead**: Execution and quality responsibility
- **Admin/Exec Officer**: Strategic oversight and escalation
- **Finance**: Billing and payment tracking

**Stakeholder Communication**:
- **Client**: Weekly/monthly as agreed
- **Team Lead**: Daily (task level) + weekly (summary)
- **Admin/Exec Officer**: Weekly project reports
- **Finance**: Monthly billing status

## Best Practices

### Scope Management
- Be explicit about what IS and IS NOT included
- Document all assumptions
- Get written client approval
- Use change control for scope modifications

### Timeline Management
- Build in buffer for unknowns
- Identify critical path items
- Update timeline weekly
- Communicate delays immediately

### Quality Assurance
- Define clear acceptance criteria upfront
- Client reviews at milestones
- Team Lead quality reviews before delivery
- Rework included in timeline planning

### Risk Management
- Identify risks early
- Create mitigation plan
- Monitor risks weekly
- Escalate if materialized

### Communication
- Proactive vs. reactive (don't wait for client to ask)
- Regular cadence (weekly or bi-weekly)
- Document everything
- Confirm verbal agreements in writing

### Team Management
- Clear task assignments
- Regular check-ins
- Remove blockers immediately
- Recognize good work

## Project Examples

### Example 1: Web Application Development
- **Duration**: 8 weeks
- **Team Size**: 1 Team Lead, 1 Senior Dev, 1 Junior Dev
- **Deliverables**: Frontend, Backend, Testing, Documentation
- **Milestones**: Week 2 (design), Week 5 (beta), Week 8 (launch)
- **Communication**: Weekly status calls with client

### Example 2: Mobile App Maintenance
- **Duration**: Ongoing monthly
- **Team Size**: 1 Senior Dev (part-time)
- **Deliverables**: Bug fixes, feature updates, support
- **Milestones**: Monthly release
- **Communication**: Monthly check-ins

## Questions & Support

- **Project Setup**: Contact Executive Officer
- **Client Issues**: Contact Admin for escalation
- **Team Questions**: Consult Team Lead
- **Scope Questions**: Refer to requirements document
