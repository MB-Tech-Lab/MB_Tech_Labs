# Dispute Resolution & Escalation Policy

## Purpose

This policy establishes a formal process for resolving conflicts, disputes, and disagreements that may arise between team members, departments, or stakeholders. It ensures fair, transparent, and timely resolution while maintaining professional relationships.

---

## Dispute Categories

### Task & Work Disputes
- Disagreement over task requirements clarification
- Dispute about task completion quality
- Disagreement over deadline feasibility
- Task assignment disputes
- Submission rejection disagreements

### Performance & Evaluation Disputes
- Disagreement with performance rating
- Dispute over compensation/stipend calculation
- Challenge to refund eligibility decision
- Complaint about evaluation fairness

### Time Tracking Disputes
- Disagreement over time entry rejection
- Dispute about flagged time entries
- Challenge to time audit findings
- Discrepancy in recorded hours

### Access & Permission Disputes
- Disagreement with access denial
- Challenge to role assignment
- Dispute over permission limitations

### Policy & Process Disputes
- Disagreement with policy interpretation
- Challenge to policy application
- Complaint about unfair treatment

### HR & Personnel Disputes
- Complaint about manager conduct
- Harassment or discrimination complaint
- Fairness of hiring/termination decision
- Complaint about performance evaluation

### Client Communication Disputes
- Dispute about project scope with client
- Disagreement over deliverables
- Conflict with client expectations

## Escalation Hierarchy

### Level 1: Direct Resolution (Peer/Team Lead)
**Participants**: Disputing parties + direct supervisor

**Process**:
1. Request meeting between parties
2. Present viewpoints calmly and professionally
3. Discuss facts and perspectives
4. Attempt to reach mutual agreement
5. Document discussion outcome
6. If resolved: Close dispute
7. If unresolved: Escalate to Level 2

**Timeline**: Initiate within 3 business days, resolve within 7 days

---

### Level 2: Manager/Supervisor Review
**Participants**: Direct supervisor + disputants (may be separate meetings)

**Process**:
1. Supervisor reviews dispute details
2. Requests documentation from both parties
3. Conducts separate interviews if needed
4. Reviews relevant policies and procedures
5. Makes preliminary decision
6. Communicates decision to parties
7. Documents reasoning and decision
8. If parties dissatisfied: Escalate to Level 3

**Timeline**: Review within 5 business days, decision within 10 days

---

### Level 3: HR/Executive Review
**Participants**: HR Manager or Executive Officer + supervisors + disputants (if needed)

**Process**:
1. HR/Executive reviews all Level 1 and 2 documentation
2. May conduct interviews or request additional information
3. Consults with relevant policies
4. Considers precedent and fairness
5. Makes level 3 decision
6. Communicates decision to all parties
7. Documents comprehensive decision record
8. If critical: Escalate to Level 4

**Timeline**: Review within 7 business days, decision within 14 days

---

### Level 4: Admin Final Review
**Participants**: Admin (final authority)

**Process**:
1. Admin reviews complete escalation trail
2. Assesses levels 1-3 decision process
3. May request additional investigation if needed
4. Makes final binding decision
5. Communicates final decision
6. Documents Admin reasoning
7. Decision is final and binding

**Timeline**: Review within 10 business days, final decision within 21 days

---

## Escalation Process

### Initiating Dispute Resolution

**Step 1: Report Dispute**
- Inform immediate supervisor or HR Manager
- Provide basic information: issue, who involved, when occurred
- Request formal dispute process
- Dispute logged with date and parties

**Step 2: Documentation**
- Both parties provide written statement
- Include facts, perspective, desired resolution
- Attach supporting documentation
- Submit within 3 business days

**Step 3: Investigation**
- Appropriate level reviews documentation
- Gathers additional information if needed
- May interview other witnesses
- Determines facts and context

**Step 4: Decision**
- Reviews all evidence
- Applies relevant policies
- Makes decision with reasoning
- Communicates to parties

**Step 5: Implementation**
- Execute decision (if action required)
- Document actions taken
- Verify resolution is effective
- Close if satisfied, escalate if not

## Common Dispute Scenarios & Resolution

### Scenario 1: Task Quality Dispute

**Situation**: Task submitted, Team Lead rejects for quality issues, Developer disagrees

**Level 1 Resolution**:
1. Team Lead and Developer discuss specific issues
2. Review acceptance criteria together
3. Developer either:
   - Agrees and revises task, OR
   - Provides documentation showing criteria met

**If Unresolved**:
- Escalate to Project Manager
- Project Manager reviews submission and criteria
- Project Manager's decision is binding at Level 2

---

### Scenario 2: Performance Rating Dispute

**Situation**: Employee receives "Satisfactory" rating, believes should be "Good"

**Level 1 Resolution**:
1. Employee discusses with Team Lead
2. Review specific metrics used
3. Team Lead explains reasoning

**If Unresolved**:
1. Escalate to Project Manager
2. Project Manager reviews raw data
3. Project Manager may:
   - Confirm rating, OR
   - Adjust rating if warranted
4. HR Manager documents outcome

---

### Scenario 3: Time Entry Dispute

**Situation**: Multiple time entries flagged as suspicious, Employee disagrees

**Level 1 Resolution**:
1. Team Lead discusses flagged entries with Employee
2. Employee provides context
3. Team Lead decides if entries valid or invalid

**If Unresolved**:
1. Project Manager reviews entries and context
2. Project Manager decides if entries valid
3. If invalid, Employee may:
   - Accept and not repeat, OR
   - Continue dispute to Level 3

**Level 3**:
1. HR Manager reviews entries
2. HR Manager decides final validity
3. If affected pay, corrective adjustment made

---

### Scenario 4: Access Denial Dispute

**Situation**: Employee denied access request, believes should have access

**Level 1 Resolution**:
1. HR Manager discusses denial reason
2. Employee may provide additional justification
3. HR Manager reconsiders

**If Unresolved**:
1. Admin reviews access policy
2. Determines if access appropriate
3. Admin decision is binding

## Dispute Documentation

### Required Documentation
- **Dispute Report**: Who, what, when, where, why
- **Party Statements**: Each party's perspective
- **Supporting Evidence**: Documents, timestamps, records
- **Investigation Notes**: Evidence gathered, witnesses interviewed
- **Decision & Rationale**: Why decision made, policy citations
- **Appeal Notes**: Why appealed (if applicable)

### Record Retention
- All dispute documentation retained for 7 years
- Accessible by involved parties
- Retained separately from personnel file (unless HR matter)
- Maintained as audit trail

## Special Categories

### Harassment or Discrimination Complaints

**Immediate Actions**:
1. Document complaint in detail
2. Report to HR Manager immediately
3. Ensure safety and support for complainant
4. Preserve all evidence

**Investigation**:
1. HR Manager and Admin initiate formal investigation
2. Maintain confidentiality (within investigation needs)
3. Interview involved parties and witnesses
4. Gather documentation
5. Determine findings

**Resolution**:
1. If substantiated: Appropriate corrective action
2. May include:
   - Disciplinary action against perpetrator
   - Mediation or restructuring
   - Termination if severe
3. Support measures for complainant
4. Prevention measures to prevent recurrence

**Protection**:
- No retaliation against complainants
- Retaliation is additional policy violation
- Confidentiality maintained as much as possible

---

### Client Communication Disputes

**Scenario**: Team member communicates with client without authorization

**Process**:
1. Project Manager discusses breach with employee
2. Explain communication policy
3. Determine if accidental or intentional

**If Unresolved**:
1. Escalate to Admin
2. Admin reviews communication and policy
3. Admin determines if violation confirmed
4. If confirmed:
   - Written warning on first offense
   - Escalating consequences for repeated offenses

---

### Interoperability Disputes (Between Departments)

**Scenario**: Project Manager and Team Lead disagree on project timeline

**Process**:
1. Direct discussion between parties
2. Review facts and constraints
3. Find compromise if possible

**If Unresolved**:
1. Escalate to Executive Officer
2. Executive Officer hears both perspectives
3. Executive Officer decides final timeline
4. Decision is binding

---

## Conflict Prevention

### Proactive Measures
- Clear policies and role definitions (reduces misunderstanding)
- Regular communication and check-ins (prevents escalation)
- Training on conflict resolution (better skills)
- Clear escalation procedures (people know process)
- Fair and transparent decision-making (increases trust)

### Early Intervention
- Supervisor notices conflict brewing
- Initiates informal discussion
- Clarifies expectations
- Addresses small issues before they grow

---

## Mediation Option

### When Mediation Can Help
- Relationship conflicts (not factual disputes)
- Communication breakdowns
- Process disagreements
- Ongoing working relationships

### Mediation Process
1. Both parties agree to mediation
2. Neutral HR Manager or external mediator
3. Confidential mediation sessions
4. Goal is mutual understanding
5. Helps parties find their own resolution
6. If mediation successful: Document agreement
7. If unsuccessful: Proceed with standard escalation

---

## Appeals Process

### Right to Appeal
- Party can appeal any level decision
- Must be filed within 5 business days of decision
- Must include new information or identify error
- Appeals reviewed by higher level

### Appeal Submission
1. Submit written appeal
2. Include: Decision appealing, reason for appeal, new information
3. Submit to next level up

### Appeal Review
- Next level reviews original decision
- Considers new information or error claims
- May uphold, modify, or reverse decision
- Communicates outcome
- Appeal decision is typically final

### Example Appeal Path
1. Level 2 Manager decision → Appeal to Level 3 Executive
2. Level 3 Executive decision → Appeal to Level 4 Admin
3. Level 4 Admin decision → Final (unless legal appeal available)

---

## Confidentiality

### During Dispute
- Minimize disclosure of dispute details
- Need-to-know basis only
- Involved parties can discuss with advisors
- Public discussion discouraged

### After Resolution
- Decision communicated to parties involved
- Broader communication made only if necessary
- Details kept confidential
- Public "need to know" vs. "nice to know"

### Records
- Complete records maintained confidentially
- Access restricted to involved parties and Admin
- Separate from general personnel record

---

## No Retaliation

**Policy**: Employees cannot be retaliated against for:
- Filing a dispute or complaint
- Participating in dispute resolution
- Providing testimony
- Following escalation procedures

**Retaliation Examples**:
- Negative performance evaluation for filing dispute
- Exclusion from projects/team for complaint
- Reduced compensation for escalation
- Hostile treatment after resolution

**Retaliation Response**:
- Report to HR Manager or Admin
- Separate investigation triggered
- Retaliation is independent policy violation
- Consequences apply to person retaliating

---

## Resolution & Binding Nature

### Authority & Finality
- Level 4 Admin decision is final and binding
- Parties accept Admin decision as resolved
- No further escalation available (unless legal grounds)
- Decision documented and implemented

### Implementation
- Party responsible for executing decision (if action required)
- Verify implementation completed
- Document completion
- Move forward professionally

### Ongoing Relationship
- Parties expected to maintain professional relationship
- Manager/supervisor may monitor relationship
- If deteriorates, further escalation may occur

## Support During Disputes

### Available Support
- HR Manager available to discuss process
- Can seek advice from supervisor (if not party)
- Can bring advisor/representative to meetings (with advance notice)
- Confidential discussion with admin (if needed)
- Employee Assistance Program (EAP) if available

### Documentation Support
- HR can help organize documentation
- HR can explain process and expectations
- Admin can discuss policy interpretation

- Common issues identified
- Policy improvements considered
- Training identified if needed

## External Resolution

### When Internal Resolution Not Possible
- If dispute cannot be resolved internally
- If legal matter involved
- If external compliance issue
- May pursue legal remedies
- May involve external mediators or arbitration

### Arbitration (If Applicable)
- Both parties agree to binding arbitration
- Arbitrator selected
- Arbitration hearing held
- Arbitration award is binding
- Document arbitration agreement and result
