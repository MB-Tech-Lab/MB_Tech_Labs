# Performance Evaluation

## Purpose

The performance evaluation system measures individual, team, and organizational productivity using objective metrics. Performance ratings drive compensation decisions (stipend eligibility), development plans, and career progression.

---

## Evaluation Framework

### Who Gets Evaluated?
- **All Employees**: Every developer, intern, and team member
- **Team Leads**: Evaluated for team leadership and execution
- **Project Managers**: Evaluated for project delivery and client satisfaction
- **All Roles**: No one exempt from evaluation

### Who Conducts Evaluations?
- **Team Lead**: Primary evaluator for team members
- **Project Manager**: Reviews Team Lead evaluations, provides context
- **HR Manager**: Processes and archives evaluations
- **Admin**: Final authority on major decisions

### Evaluation Frequency
- **Real-Time Tracking**: Metrics collected daily
- **Weekly Performance Data**: Weekly summaries generated
- **Monthly Performance Ratings**: Official ratings issued
- **Quarterly Reviews**: Performance trends analyzed
- **Annual Evaluation**: Comprehensive year-end review

## Key Performance Metrics

### 1. Task Completion Rate
**What it measures**: Percentage of assigned tasks completed

**Calculation**:
```
Completed Tasks / Total Assigned Tasks × 100
```

**Target by Rating**:
- Excellent: 95%+
- Good: 85-94%
- Satisfactory: 75-84%
- Needs Improvement: <75%

**Includes**:
- Tasks completed and approved
- Excludes incomplete/rejected tasks

### 2. On-Time Delivery Rate
**What it measures**: Percentage of tasks submitted by deadline

**Calculation**:
```
On-Time Submissions / Total Submissions × 100
```

**Target by Rating**:
- Excellent: 95%+
- Good: 85-94%
- Satisfactory: 75-84%
- Needs Improvement: <75%

**Considers**:
- Actual submission date vs. deadline
- Legitimate delays (approved extensions)
- Blocked tasks (may be excluded)

### 3. Quality Score
**What it measures**: Submission approval rate on first review

**Calculation**:
```
First-Submit Approvals / Total Submissions × 100
```

**Target by Rating**:
- Excellent: <5% rejection rate (95%+ first-submit approval)
- Good: <10% rejection rate (90%+ first-submit approval)
- Satisfactory: <20% rejection rate (80%+ first-submit approval)
- Needs Improvement: >20% rejection rate

**Tracked**:
- Rejections for quality issues
- Number of revisions needed
- Reviewer feedback indicators
- Client satisfaction feedback

### 4. Consistency
**What it measures**: Predictable, regular work performance

**Indicators**:
- Daily time logging (8 hours/day target)
- Consistent daily performance
- Minimal gaps or irregular patterns
- Reliable presence during work hours
- Stable task completion week to week

**Target by Rating**:
- Excellent: 95%+ consistent 8-hour days
- Good: 90%+ consistent 8-hour days
- Satisfactory: 80%+ consistent 8-hour days
- Needs Improvement: <80% consistency

### 5. Attendance & Engagement
**What it measures**: Presence and participation

**Indicators**:
- Present during work hours
- Participates in team meetings
- Responsive and engaged
- No unexplained absences
- Proactive communication

**Target by Rating**:
- Excellent: Excellent attendance, highly engaged
- Good: Good attendance, engaged
- Satisfactory: Acceptable attendance, minimally engaged
- Needs Improvement: Poor attendance or disengagement

## Performance Ratings

### Rating Levels & Definitions

#### Excellent (90-100%)
**Definition**: Consistently high performance, exceeds expectations

**Characteristics**:
- 95%+ task completion
- 95%+ on-time delivery
- <5% rejection rate
- 95%+ time logging consistency
- Excellent attendance
- Takes initiative
- Helps team members
- Proactive problem-solving
- High-quality deliverables

**Behaviors**:
- Goes beyond assigned scope
- Mentors others
- Identifies improvements
- Communicates proactively
- Meets all commitments
- Adapts well to changes

#### Good (75-89%)
**Definition**: Meets expectations consistently, solid performer

**Characteristics**:
- 85%+ task completion
- 85%+ on-time delivery
- 10-15% occasional issues
- 90%+ time logging consistency
- Good attendance
- Reliable contributor
- Quality work with minor revisions
- Meets most commitments

**Behaviors**:
- Completes assignments well
- Asks questions appropriately
- Works well with team
- Generally communicates
- Reliable and dependable
- Steady performer

#### Satisfactory (60-74%)
**Definition**: Meets minimum expectations, needs improvement in some areas

**Characteristics**:
- 75%+ task completion
- 75%+ on-time delivery
- 15-20% requiring rework
- 80%+ time logging consistency
- Acceptable attendance (occasional issues)
- Completes basic requirements
- Some quality concerns
- Periodic delays

**Behaviors**:
- Does what's asked
- Occasional gaps or issues
- Inconsistent engagement
- May miss deadlines
- Minimum quality standards
- Requires supervision

#### Needs Improvement (<60%)
**Definition**: Does not meet expectations, significant concerns

**Characteristics**:
- <75% task completion
- <75% on-time delivery
- >20% rejection/rework rate
- <80% time logging consistency
- Attendance problems
- Major quality issues
- Regular delays
- Not meeting basic requirements

**Behaviors**:
- Frequent missed deadlines
- Quality concerns
- Poor attendance/engagement
- Requires constant supervision
- Not meeting standards
- Performance improvement needed

## Evaluation Process

### Monthly Evaluation Cycle

**Week 1 (Collection)**:
- System automatically calculates metrics
- Data compiled from task system
- Time tracking aggregated
- Quality scores calculated

**Week 2 (Review)**:
- Team Lead reviews data
- Assesses qualitative factors
- Considers context and circumstances
- Determines preliminary rating

**Week 3 (Discussion)**:
- Team Lead meets with team member
- Discusses metrics and rating
- Explains reasoning
- Provides feedback
- Listens to member's perspective
- Documents discussion

**Week 4 (Finalization)**:
- Final rating issued
- Communicated to HR
- Archived in system
- Used for metrics tracking
- Feeds into compensation decisions

### Evaluation Meeting

**Preparation**:
- Team Lead reviews all metrics
- Prepares specific examples
- Plans constructive feedback
- Identifies strengths and improvements

**Meeting Agenda**:
1. Review metrics (5 min)
2. Discuss performance (10 min)
3. Explain rating (5 min)
4. Listen to member input (5 min)
5. Discuss next steps (5 min)

**Tone**:
- Constructive and supportive
- Specific (use examples)
- Fair and objective
- Respectful
- Focused on improvement

### Documentation
- Meeting notes recorded
- Rating documented
- Feedback captured
- Improvement plan (if applicable)
- Member can add comments
- Stored in personnel record

## Performance Factors & Context

### Factors That May Affect Metrics

**Positive Context**:
- Learned new technology (metrics may improve)
- Helped team with unplanned work
- Took on mentoring responsibilities
- Completed high-priority rush items
- Overcame challenges/obstacles

**Negative Context**:
- Illness or personal emergency
- Dependent on other team's work
- Blocked on dependencies
- Team/project turbulence
- Resource constraints
- Learning new area

**Consideration**:
- Team Lead accounts for context
- Not excuse, but explanation
- Adjusted expectations if needed
- Documented reasoning
- Transparent communication

## Stipend Eligibility

Stipend allocations are determined by performance rating:

### Excellent (90-100%)
- **Stipend**: 100% of maximum range
- **Eligibility**: Full
- **Bonus**: Possible for exceptional work
- **Review**: Eligible for increased allocation

### Good (75-89%)
- **Stipend**: 80-90% of standard range
- **Eligibility**: Full
- **Bonus**: Standard performance
- **Review**: Stable allocation

### Satisfactory (60-74%)
- **Stipend**: 50-70% reduced range
- **Eligibility**: Conditional (partial)
- **Bonus**: Not eligible
- **Review**: Contingent on improvement

### Needs Improvement (<60%)
- **Stipend**: 0-30% minimal
- **Eligibility**: Minimal or none
- **Bonus**: Not eligible
- **Review**: Performance improvement plan required

## Performance Improvement Plans (PIP)

### When PIP is Recommended
- Rating drops to Satisfactory or below
- Chronic issues not improving
- Specific behavioral concerns
- Performance goals not met

### PIP Contents
- **Specific Goals**: Clear, measurable targets
- **Timeline**: 30-60 day improvement period
- **Support**: Resources and coaching provided
- **Check-ins**: Frequent progress reviews (weekly)
- **Success Criteria**: How to exit PIP (back to Satisfactory or above)

### PIP Process
1. HR Manager and Team Lead create plan
2. Member informed and accepts (or escalates)
3. Member works toward goals with support
4. Weekly check-ins on progress
5. After 30-60 days, evaluate
6. If successful: Exit PIP, resume normal reviews
7. If unsuccessful: Further action considered

## Stipend Eligibility

Stipend allocations (for interns) are based on performance ratings:

### Excellent Performance (90-100%)
- Full stipend eligibility
- Up to maximum stipend range
- Possible bonus for exceptional work

### Good Performance (75-89%)
- Full stipend eligibility
- Standard range

### Satisfactory Performance (60-74%)
- Partial stipend eligibility
- Reduced range
- Contingent on improvement

### Needs Improvement (<60%)
- Minimal or no stipend
- Requires performance improvement plan
- Stipend may be withheld until improvement

## Refund Eligibility

Refund eligibility (for program fees) is based on:
- Complete internship duration
- Satisfactory overall performance (60%+)
- No policy violations
- Acceptable attendance and time tracking

**Disqualifiers**:
- Fake or inflated time entries
- Prolonged inactivity (>3 consecutive days)
- Policy violations or misconduct
- Failure to complete program commitments

### Daily Checks (Team Lead)

- Flag issues immediately

### Weekly Review (Team Lead & Manager)

- Comprehensive performance review
